\section{Introduction}
RAFAELIA is a continuous-variable processor over $\mathbb{T}^7=(\mathbb{R}/\mathbb{Z})^7$ designed for deterministic control firmware and self-verification every canonical period of $42$ steps.

\section{Mathematical Model}
State: $\mathbf{s}=(u,v,\psi,\chi,\rho,\delta,\sigma) \in [0,1)^7$.

EMA dynamics:
\begin{align}
C_{t+1}&=0.75C_t+0.25C_{in},\\
H_{t+1}&=0.75H_t+0.25H_{in},\\
\phi_t&=(1-H_t)C_t.
\end{align}

Forward recurrence (Q16.16):
\begin{equation}
F_{n+1}=F_n\frac{\sqrt{3}}{2}-\pi\sin(279^\circ),\quad x_{n+42}=x_n.
\end{equation}

\section{Hardware Architecture}
1000 active oscillators + 8 special points in a 1008-cell control map; BitRAF words (42-bit) feed phase/amplitude DAC chains.

\section{Error Correction and Commit Gate}
Pipeline phases: LOAD $\rightarrow$ PROCESS $\rightarrow$ VERIFY $\rightarrow$ COMMIT. If VERIFY fails (XOR/CRC mismatch), rollback is asserted to optical delay-line snapshot.

\section{Programming Model: ISA and BitRAF}
BitRAF fields: frequency, weight, phase, partial CRC, load, opcode. One word is emitted each cycle at 100 MHz.

\section{Comparison and Estimates}
Compared with gate-model qubit processors and annealers, RAFAELIA adds cycle-level self-verification and deterministic fixed-point control.
At 100 MHz, 42-step epoch duration is 420 ns; verify checkpoints every 7 cycles occur every 70 ns.
